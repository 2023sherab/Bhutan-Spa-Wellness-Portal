FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTION

Thai massage is a traditional healing practice from Thailand that combines yoga, Ayurveda, and Chinese medicine. Practitioners use hands, elbows, knees, and feet to apply pressure and perform stretches on a padded mat. It's known for improving flexibility, relieving tension, and promoting relaxation.

Traditional Bhutanese Hot Stone Bath: This is a popular spa treatment in Bhutan, where hot stones are immersed in water infused with aromatic herbs and then placed in a wooden tub. The bath is believed to have therapeutic effects, promoting relaxation, detoxification, and relief from muscle tension.

Thai Massage: Originating in Thailand, Thai massage incorporates elements of yoga, Ayurveda, and traditional Chinese medicine. It involves deep tissue massage, passive stretching, and joint manipulation techniques performed on a padded mat on the floor. Body Massage: Body massage is a broader term that encompasses various massage techniques from different cultures. It typically involves manipulating muscles and soft tissues of the body using hands, fingers, elbows, or specialized tools. Techniques can vary widely depending on the type of body massage, such as Swedish, deep tissue, or hot stone massage.
